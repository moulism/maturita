Education System

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • Czech school system: nursery → primary (6–15) → secondary (grammar/vocational) → university
  • UK system: primary → secondary → sixth form (A-levels) → university (3 yrs, Bachelor's)
  • GCSE at 16 (general), A-levels at 18 (university entry qualification)
  • Our school: VOŠ a SPŠE Plzeň, IT AVAS, technical grammar school + college
  • Key vocab: compulsory education, curriculum, tuition fees, scholarship, graduate, undergraduate

About our school – what to say

  • I attend VOŠ a SPŠE Plzeň – a technical grammar school and college combined.
  • My class is IT AVAS – we specialize in IT, data analytics, databases, and networking.
  • The school year has two semesters. We have a maturita exam in May.
  • My favourite subjects are programming and databases because I enjoy solving logical problems.
  • After graduation I plan to study at a university or start working in IT.

My personal qualities as a student

  • I am analytical – I like breaking problems into smaller parts.
  • I am hardworking but I could be more organized with deadlines.
  • I work well in a team and I am good at explaining technical things simply.
⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • What do you think about tuition fees? – In Czech Republic university is mostly free, which gives equal opportunity. In the UK fees are high but graduates often earn more, so it can pay off.
  • Would you like to study abroad? – Yes/No + reason. E.g. Erasmus programme, experience new culture, improve English.
  • What is the difference between GCSE and A-levels? – GCSE at 16 = broad general education, all students take it. A-levels at 18 = specialised subjects for university entry.
  • What would you change about your school? – More practical IT projects, more English, less theory.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes Start with the general structure of the Czech school system, then compare it to the UK. Then talk personally: describe your school, your class, your favourite subjects and why. Mention your personal qualities as a student – both strengths and weaknesses (examiners like honesty). End with your plans after graduation.
