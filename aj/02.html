UK and Ireland

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • UK = 4 countries: England, Scotland, Wales, Northern Ireland; capital London
  • Ireland (Eire) = separate republic; capital Dublin; official languages Irish + English
  • Government: constitutional monarchy, King Charles III, Parliament (House of Commons + Lords)
  • Currency: British pound (GBP); Ireland uses Euro
  • Famous landmarks: Big Ben (Elizabeth Tower), Tower of London, Stonehenge, Edinburgh Castle
  • Traditions: Bonfire Night (Nov 5), Guy Fawkes, Highland Games, St. Patrick's Day

Famous sights

Big Ben
The Elizabeth Tower clock at the Houses of Parliament, London. Built 1859.
Tower of London
11th century fortress; home of the Crown Jewels; Beefeater guards.
Stonehenge
Prehistoric stone circle in Wiltshire (~3000 BC). Purpose still debated.
Edinburgh Castle
Fortress on volcanic rock; houses Scottish Crown Jewels.
Oxford & Cambridge
World's oldest and most prestigious universities (founded 12th–13th century).

Traditions and culture

  • Guy Fawkes / Bonfire Night (November 5) – fireworks, bonfires; commemorates failed Gunpowder Plot (1605)
  • St. Patrick's Day (March 17) – Irish national holiday; parades, green colour, shamrock
  • Highland Games – Scottish traditions: tossing the caber, bagpipes
  • The Proms – summer classical music festival at Royal Albert Hall, London
  • Afternoon tea – tea with scones, sandwiches; a British social tradition

Famous British people

  • William Shakespeare (playwright), Isaac Newton (physicist), Charles Darwin (biologist)
  • Queen Victoria, Winston Churchill (WWII PM), Margaret Thatcher (first female PM)
  • The Beatles (Liverpool band), David Beckham (footballer), J.K. Rowling (Harry Potter)
⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • What is the difference between Great Britain and the UK? – Great Britain = England + Scotland + Wales (the island). UK = Great Britain + Northern Ireland. The British Isles = UK + Ireland + smaller islands.
  • Why does the UK use pounds and not euros? – The UK never joined the Eurozone. They kept the pound as a symbol of independence. After Brexit (2020) they also left the EU single market.
  • What do you know about Brexit? – In 2016 the UK voted to leave the EU (52% Leave). They officially left on 31 January 2020. It caused major debates about trade, immigration, and Northern Ireland border.
  • Would you like to visit the UK? Why? – Personal answer + specific place you would visit and why.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes Start with the basic geography – UK = 4 countries. Then describe the government briefly (constitutional monarchy, Parliament). Move to famous sights – pick 2–3 and say something interesting about each. Mention traditions you find interesting. End with something personal: a place you would like to visit or a famous British person you find inspiring.
