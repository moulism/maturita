North America – USA and Canada

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • USA: 50 states, capital Washington D.C., ~335 million people, federal republic
  • Canada: 2nd largest country, bilingual (English + French), capital Ottawa
  • US government: President, Congress (Senate + House of Representatives)
  • Famous sights: Statue of Liberty, Grand Canyon, Niagara Falls, Golden Gate Bridge
  • Thanksgiving (4th Thursday November), Independence Day (July 4th)
  • Canada: maple leaf symbol, Hockey, Tim Hortons, multicultural

Famous sights

Statue of Liberty
Gift from France (1886), symbol of freedom and democracy. New York Harbor.
Grand Canyon
277-mile long gorge carved by Colorado River. Arizona.
Niagara Falls
On the US–Canada border. Three waterfalls – most famous: Horseshoe Falls (Canada side).
Golden Gate Bridge
Iconic suspension bridge in San Francisco (1937). Dark orange colour.
Mount Rushmore
Granite sculpture of 4 presidents: Washington, Jefferson, Roosevelt, Lincoln. South Dakota.

Holidays and traditions

  • Independence Day (July 4) – US national holiday; celebrates 1776 Declaration of Independence; fireworks, BBQ
  • Thanksgiving (4th Thursday in November in USA; 2nd Monday in October in Canada) – turkey, family gathering, gratitude
  • Halloween (October 31) – trick or treat, costumes, pumpkin carving; Celtic origins
  • Super Bowl Sunday – American football final; biggest annual sports event in the US

Famous Americans / Canadians

  • Abraham Lincoln (16th president, abolished slavery), Martin Luther King Jr. (civil rights)
  • Thomas Edison, Neil Armstrong (first man on the Moon, 1969)
  • Canada: Justin Trudeau (PM), Celine Dion, Drake, Ryan Gosling
⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • What is the capital of the USA / Canada? – USA: Washington D.C. (NOT New York!). Canada: Ottawa (NOT Toronto!). Classic trick questions.
  • What do you know about the American Dream? – The belief that anyone can succeed through hard work regardless of where they were born. Today debated – studies show social mobility in the US is actually lower than in many European countries.
  • What are the differences between the USA and Canada? – Canada: universal healthcare, bilingual, constitutional monarchy, more liberal immigration. USA: private healthcare, more individualistic culture, republic.
  • Have you ever been to North America? – If yes, describe your experience. If no, say which state/city you would most like to visit and why.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes Cover USA first (government, landmarks, holidays) then Canada briefly (bilingual, Ottawa, maple syrup). Pick 2–3 sights and explain something interesting about each. Mention at least one holiday. A good structure: geography → government → famous places → culture/holidays → personal connection.
