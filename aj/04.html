Australia and New Zealand

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • Australia: 6th largest country, ~27 million people, capital Canberra (not Sydney!)
  • New Zealand: 2 main islands, capital Wellington (not Auckland!), Maori culture
  • Both: constitutional monarchies (King Charles III), Commonwealth members
  • Unique species: kangaroo, koala, platypus, kiwi (NZ), wombat
  • Great Barrier Reef: world's largest coral reef, off Queensland coast
  • Anzac Day (April 25): remembers WWI soldiers from Australia and New Zealand

Unique wildlife

Kangaroo
Australia's national symbol. Marsupial – carries young in pouch. Can jump 3m high.
Koala
Arboreal marsupial; eats only eucalyptus leaves. Not actually a bear.
Platypus
Egg-laying mammal with duck-bill. One of the world's most unusual animals.
Kiwi (NZ)
Flightless nocturnal bird; New Zealand's national symbol. Also the informal name for New Zealanders.
Wombat
Burrowing marsupial; produces cube-shaped droppings (unique in animal kingdom).

Famous sights and places

  • Great Barrier Reef – World's largest coral reef (~2,300 km). Off Queensland coast. UNESCO World Heritage Site. Threatened by climate change and bleaching.
  • Sydney Opera House – Iconic landmark designed by Jørn Utzon (1973). UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Uluru (Ayers Rock) – Sacred sandstone monolith in the Northern Territory. Important to Aboriginal Australians.
  • Fiordland NZ – Milford Sound, dramatic fjords. Film location for Lord of the Rings.

Traditions and holidays

  • Australia Day (January 26) – national holiday; controversial for Aboriginal peoples (called Invasion Day by some)
  • Anzac Day (April 25) – remembers soldiers who fought at Gallipoli (WWI) – most significant day of remembrance
  • Haka (NZ) – traditional Maori war dance; performed by the All Blacks rugby team before matches
⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • What is the capital of Australia / New Zealand? – Australia: Canberra. NZ: Wellington. Classic trick – everyone says Sydney or Auckland.
  • Why are seasons reversed in Australia? – Australia is in the Southern Hemisphere. When the Northern Hemisphere tilts toward the sun (June–August = our summer), Australia tilts away – it's winter there.
  • What environmental problems does the Great Barrier Reef face? – Coral bleaching from rising sea temperatures, ocean acidification, agricultural runoff, crown-of-thorns starfish.
  • What is unique about Australian wildlife? – Australia was isolated for millions of years – marsupials (kangaroo, koala) and monotremes (platypus) evolved there and almost nowhere else.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes Start with basic geography – the classic trick question about capitals. Describe the unique wildlife – it always impresses examiners and gives you plenty to talk about. Mention the Great Barrier Reef and climate change connection. End with Anzac Day or Aboriginal culture for depth.
