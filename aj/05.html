Job and Career

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • Job hunting: CV, cover letter, job interview, references
  • Types of employment: full-time, part-time, freelance, self-employed, internship
  • Qualities: hardworking, reliable, team player, creative, analytical, good communicator
  • My future career: IT / data analytics / programming
  • Key vocab: salary/wage, promotion, resignation, redundancy, maternity leave

Key vocabulary

CV / résumé
Document listing education, experience, skills. In the UK: CV. In the USA: résumé.
cover letter
Motivační dopis – why you want the job and why you are the best candidate.
job interview
Pohovor – meeting with the employer to assess your suitability.
salary / wage
Salary = monthly fixed pay (managers, office workers). Wage = hourly pay (manual work).
promotion
Povýšení – moving to a higher position.
redundancy
Nadbytečnost – losing a job because the position no longer exists.
freelance
Self-employed, working for multiple clients. Common in IT, design, writing.
internship
Stáž – work experience placement, often unpaid or low-paid.

Job hunting process

  • Search for jobs: job portals (LinkedIn, Indeed, Jobs.cz), company websites, recruitment agencies
  • Write a CV: personal details, education, work experience, skills, hobbies
  • Write a cover letter: 3 paragraphs – why this company, what you offer, request for interview
  • Job interview: dress professionally, research the company, prepare questions to ask
  • Common interview questions: "Tell me about yourself", "What are your strengths/weaknesses?", "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"

Personal qualities for IT career

  • I am analytical – I enjoy breaking complex problems into smaller parts
  • I am detail-oriented – important in programming and data work
  • I am a quick learner – technology changes fast, continuous learning is essential
  • I work well both independently and in a team
  • I have good communication skills – I can explain technical things to non-technical people

My future career

  • After graduation I would like to work in data analytics / software development / IT
  • Possibly study at university first to deepen my knowledge
  • I would prefer working for a tech company or a firm with strong IT department
  • Long-term: possibly move into a senior or team lead position
⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • What is the difference between salary and wage? – Salary is a fixed monthly or annual amount (white-collar jobs). Wage is paid by the hour (manual/part-time workers).
  • What would be your dream job? – Describe specifically: what field, what kind of work, what company, why.
  • What skills do you think are most important in the IT industry? – Technical (programming, databases, cloud), soft skills (communication, problem-solving, adaptability).
  • Have you had any work experience? – Describe any job, volunteering, or school project that counts as experience. Focus on what you learned.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes Describe the job hunting process step by step: CV → cover letter → interview. Then talk about your own qualities and skills – use specific examples from school or projects. End with your personal future career plans. Examiners love when students can connect the topic to their own life.
