Holidays and Feast Days

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • Czech holidays: Christmas (Dec 24–26), Easter, St. Nicholas (Dec 5), carnival
  • UK holidays: Christmas, Boxing Day (Dec 26), Guy Fawkes, Easter
  • USA: Thanksgiving, Halloween, Independence Day (July 4), Christmas
  • My favourite holiday: Christmas / Easter + personal tradition
  • Key vocab: carol, advent, custom, feast, tradition, fireworks, costume

Useful vocabulary

tradition / custom
Something done regularly within a culture. It's a tradition to eat carp on Christmas Eve in the Czech Republic.
carol
Vánoční píseň. We sang Christmas carols in front of the church.
feast
A large special meal. Christmas is a feast day.
advent
The four weeks before Christmas. We light one Advent candle each Sunday.
⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • How do Czech Easter traditions differ from Western ones? – In the West Easter is mainly about chocolate eggs and the Easter Bunny. In Czech Republic boys whip girls with a willow switch (pomlázka) – a pre-Christian fertility ritual.
  • Do you think Halloween should be celebrated in CZ? – Present both sides: it's fun / commercial American import. Many Czechs prefer to keep traditional Dušičky (All Souls' Day).
  • What is Boxing Day? – British holiday on Dec 26. Originally employers gave Christmas boxes (gifts) to servants. Now mainly sales shopping and sports events.
  • What is your favourite holiday and why? – Personal answer with specific family tradition detail.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes Compare Czech and English-speaking country traditions – examiners love this comparison. Describe Christmas in Czech Republic in detail (it's very specific and shows you know your culture). Then compare with UK or USA. End with your personal favourite holiday and a specific memory or tradition from your family.
