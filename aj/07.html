Czech Republic

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • Central Europe, ~10.9 million people, capital Prague, EU member since 2004
  • Government: parliamentary republic; President + Prime Minister + Parliament (Chamber + Senate)
  • Currency: Czech Koruna (CZK), NOT euro
  • Famous for: castles (Český Krumlov, Karlštejn), beer, Pilsner lager (from Plzeň!)
  • Key historical events: 1918 independence, 1938 Munich, 1948 communism, 1968 Prague Spring, 1989 Velvet Revolution

History – key events

  • Great Moravia (9th century) – first major Slavic state; St. Cyril and Methodius brought Christianity and the Glagolitic script
  • Kingdom of Bohemia – Charles IV (1316–1378) – Holy Roman Emperor; built Charles Bridge, founded Charles University (1348, oldest in Central Europe)
  • 1618–1648 Thirty Years' War – started with Defenestration of Prague
  • 1918 – Czechoslovakia founded; T.G. Masaryk as first president (October 28)
  • 1938 Munich Agreement – Western powers gave Sudetenland to Hitler
  • 1948 – Communist coup; 40 years of communist rule
  • 1968 Prague Spring – Alexander Dubček's "socialism with a human face"; crushed by Soviet invasion (August 21)
  • 1989 Velvet Revolution – peaceful end to communism; Václav Havel became president
  • 1993 – Czechoslovakia peacefully split into Czech Republic and Slovakia ("Velvet Divorce")
  • 2004 – joined the EU

Famous sights and places

Prague
Prague Castle (largest castle complex in the world), Charles Bridge, Old Town Square with Astronomical Clock (Orloj)
Český Krumlov
UNESCO town in South Bohemia; picturesque castle above river Vltava
Karlštejn
Gothic castle near Prague; built by Charles IV to keep the crown jewels
Kutná Hora
UNESCO silver mining town; Bone Church (Sedlec Ossuary) with decorations made from human bones

Czech cuisine and culture

  • Traditional food: svíčková (beef in cream sauce), vepřo-knedlo-zelo, bramboráky (potato pancakes)
  • Czech Republic has the highest beer consumption per capita in the world. Pilsner lager was invented in Plzeň in 1842.
  • Famous Czechs: Franz Kafka (writer, Prague), Antonín Dvořák (composer), Alfons Mucha (Art Nouveau artist), Martina Navrátilová (tennis)
⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • Why does the Czech Republic use Koruna and not Euro? – CZ is in the EU but has not adopted the Euro yet. There is no fixed date. Polls show most Czechs prefer to keep the Koruna.
  • What is the Velvet Revolution? – November 1989 – peaceful protests ended communist rule. Named 'velvet' because there was no violence. Václav Havel became president.
  • What is Prague Spring? – 1968 – communist reformer Dubček tried to introduce 'socialism with a human face' – more freedom. Crushed by Soviet invasion on August 21.
  • What would you recommend to a tourist visiting the Czech Republic? – Prague (castle, Charles Bridge), Český Krumlov, try svíčková, visit a brewery.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes Describe the geography briefly, then move to history – pick 3 key events and explain why they matter (1918 independence, 1968, 1989). Describe Prague and 1–2 other places. Mention food and beer (Pilsner from Plzeň specifically – shows local knowledge). End with a personal recommendation for tourists.
