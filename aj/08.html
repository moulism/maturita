Pilsen

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • Plzeň: 4th largest Czech city (~175,000), capital of Plzeňský kraj
  • Famous for: Pilsner Urquell brewery (1842), Škoda (engineering), European Capital of Culture 2015
  • Founded: 1295 by King Wenceslas II; liberation by American army (May 6, 1945)
  • Sights: St. Bartholomew's Cathedral (tallest spire in CZ), Great Synagogue (3rd largest in the world)
  • University: University of West Bohemia (ZČU)

History

  • Founded 1295 as a royal city – location at the confluence of four rivers (Mže, Radbuza, Úhlava, Úslava → Berounka)
  • Medieval importance: trade, crafts, St. Bartholomew's Cathedral construction began in 14th century
  • 1842 – First Pilsner lager brewed by Josef Groll (Bavarian brewer). Pilsner Urquell brewery founded – changed beer history worldwide
  • 1945 – Liberated by the US Army (General Patton's 3rd Army) on May 6. This is celebrated every year as Liberation Festival
  • 2015 – European Capital of Culture (alongside Mons, Belgium)

Famous sights

St. Bartholomew's Cathedral
Gothic cathedral on náměstí Republiky. Tower is 102.6 m tall – tallest church spire in Czech Republic.
Great Synagogue
Second largest synagogue in Europe and third largest in the world. Built 1892.
Pilsner Urquell Brewery
Founded 1842. You can tour underground cellars. The beer that gave its name to "pilsner" style.
Underground tunnels
Medieval tunnel network under the city centre; ~17 km of passages. Used for storage and defence.
Techmania Science Centre
Interactive science museum near Škoda; great for families.

Transport and education

  • Train: Plzeň to Prague ~1h30 (express); main station is Plzeň hlavní nádraží
  • Tram network: 3 lines; D1 motorway to Prague
  • University of West Bohemia (ZČU) – ~12,000 students; faculties include IT, engineering, law, arts
  • Our school (VOŠ a SPŠE) is on Koterovská – one of the best technical schools in the region
⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • Why is Pilsen historically significant for beer? – In 1842 Josef Groll brewed the world's first pale lager in Plzeň. Before that all beers were dark. Pilsner style is now the world's most popular beer style (Lager, Heineken, Budweiser all descended from it).
  • Why was Pilsen liberated by Americans and not Soviets? – Western Bohemia was in the US zone of operations. The US Army stopped at Plzeň per the Yalta agreement – the rest of Czechoslovakia was liberated by Soviets.
  • What would you show a foreign visitor in Plzeň? – Cathedral tower view, brewery tour, underground tunnels, Great Synagogue. Personal touch: a café or restaurant you like.
  • What do you like and dislike about living in Plzeň? – Likes: good size (not too big), beer culture, close to Prague, natural surroundings. Dislikes: fewer opportunities than Prague, public transport could be better.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes Start with location and basic facts. Tell the story of 1842 and Pilsner Urquell – it's an interesting story that every Brit/American will find fascinating. Mention the 1945 US liberation – unique connection to American history. Describe 2–3 sights you have personally visited. End with something personal about living here.
