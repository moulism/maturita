Problems of Today's World

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • Poverty: ~700 million people on <$2.15/day; causes: lack of education, corruption, war
  • Climate change: CO2 emissions, greenhouse effect, 1.5°C target (Paris Agreement 2015)
  • Terrorism and conflicts: extremism, refugee crisis, geopolitical tensions
  • Overpopulation: 8 billion+ people; strain on food, water, healthcare
  • Impact of technology: social media addiction, misinformation, job automation

Useful vocabulary

greenhouse effect
Skleníkový efekt – CO2 traps heat in atmosphere. Leads to global warming.
refugee
Uprchlík – forced to flee home due to war, persecution, or natural disaster.
corruption
Korupce – dishonest use of power for personal gain.
sustainable
Udržitelný – meeting current needs without compromising future generations.
inequality
Nerovnost – gap between rich and poor, or different groups in society.
⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • What do you think is the biggest problem in the world today? – Climate change / poverty / conflicts. Give a reason. Examiners want YOUR opinion, not just facts.
  • What can ordinary people do about climate change? – Reduce flying, eat less meat, use public transport, vote for green policies, support sustainable businesses.
  • Is technology making the world better or worse? – Both sides. Better: medicine, communication. Worse: misinformation, addiction, job losses from automation.
  • Should rich countries help poor countries more? – Yes: moral obligation, global stability. No debate: it should be about better trade rules, not just aid.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes Pick 2–3 problems and go deep rather than listing all of them. For each: what is the problem, why does it exist, what are the consequences, and what solutions are being tried. Always share your personal opinion – examiners specifically ask 'What do you think?'. Connect to personal actions you take or could take.
