Environment

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • Air pollution: greenhouse gases (CO2, CH4), smog, acid rain
  • Water pollution: plastic waste, industrial discharge, oil spills
  • Waste: 2 billion tonnes/year globally; recycling, composting, reduce-reuse-recycle
  • Solutions: renewable energy (solar, wind, hydro), electric cars, green building
  • Paris Agreement (2015): limit warming to 1.5°C; COP climate conferences
  • My contribution: recycling, less plastic, public transport, vegetarian diet

Greenhouse effect explained

  • The sun's radiation heats the Earth's surface
  • Earth radiates heat back as infrared radiation
  • Greenhouse gases (CO2, methane, water vapour) trap some of this heat – natural process
  • Human activity increases CO2 significantly (burning fossil fuels, deforestation)
  • Result: global temperatures rise → melting glaciers, rising sea levels, extreme weather

Solutions

Renewable energy
Solar, wind, hydro, geothermal. No CO2 emissions during operation.
Recycling
Reduces landfill and saves raw materials. 3Rs: Reduce, Reuse, Recycle.
Electric vehicles
No exhaust emissions. Still need clean electricity to be truly green.
Paris Agreement
2015 international treaty: limit warming to 1.5°C above pre-industrial levels. 196 countries signed.
Veganism / plant-based diet
Animal agriculture produces ~14.5% of global greenhouse emissions. Reducing meat consumption helps.

Environmental problems in CZ

  • Air quality: coal power plants (Počerady, Prunéřov) – improving but still an issue
  • Drought: Czech Republic experienced severe droughts 2018–2020; water management improving
  • Bark beetle: forests (especially Šumava) devastated by bark beetle due to warmer temperatures
  • Recycling rate: Czech Republic sorts waste well; coloured containers common in all towns
⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • What is the difference between weather and climate? – Weather = current atmospheric conditions (today it's raining). Climate = long-term average weather patterns (Czech Republic has a temperate continental climate). Climate change = long-term shift in these patterns.
  • Do you think individuals can make a difference? – Individual actions matter but systemic change (policy, industry regulation) is more important. Both are necessary. Individual choices also signal demand to companies.
  • Should developing countries be allowed to pollute more to develop their economies? – Controversial. Rich countries developed by polluting; now they ask poorer countries to use expensive clean energy. Principle of 'common but differentiated responsibilities'.
  • What do you personally do for the environment? – Recycling, using public transport, reducing meat, avoiding single-use plastic – be specific and honest.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes Explain the greenhouse effect step by step – it shows you understand the science. Then cover the main types of pollution briefly. Spend time on solutions – examiners like constructive answers. Always end with what YOU personally do – even small things count. Show you care.
