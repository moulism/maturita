Traffic and Transport

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • Means of transport: car, bus, train, tram, metro, bicycle, plane, ship
  • Advantages: car (flexible, door-to-door), train (fast, comfortable for long trips)
  • Traffic problems: congestion, road accidents, pollution, road rage
  • Traffic rules: speed limits (50 km/h in city, 90 on road, 130 motorway), seatbelts, drink-driving
  • Eco-friendly: electric cars, cycling, public transport, carpooling

Means of transport – comparison

TransportAdvantagesDisadvantages
CarDoor-to-door, flexible, fast in rural areasTraffic jams, expensive (fuel, insurance), pollutes
TrainFast on long distances, comfortable, eco-friendly, no congestionFixed schedule, can be delayed, no door-to-door
Bus/TramCheap, good for cities, reduces congestionSlower, fixed routes, crowded at peak times
BicycleZero emissions, healthy, cheap, no parking issuesWeather dependent, dangerous in traffic, slow
PlaneFast for long distancesHigh emissions, expensive, check-in time, airports outside cities

Traffic problems and solutions

  • Congestion: too many cars → solutions: park & ride, congestion charge (London), better public transport
  • Road accidents: in Czech Republic ~500 deaths/year; mostly speeding and drunk driving
  • Pollution: transport = ~20% of EU CO2 emissions → solutions: electric vehicles, cycling infrastructure
  • Road rage: aggressive driving behaviour; related to stress and anonymity in cars

Czech traffic rules – key facts

Speed limits
50 km/h in towns, 90 km/h on roads, 130 km/h on motorways (dálnice)
Alcohol limit
Zero tolerance in CZ (0.0 ‰). UK limit: 0.8 ‰.
Seatbelts
Compulsory for all passengers. Children under 150 cm must use a child seat.
Winter tyres
Required in CZ from November 1 in snowy/icy conditions (no fixed dates like in Germany).

Personal experience

  • I usually travel by bus/tram/bicycle to school – it's cheaper and better for the environment
  • For trips to Prague: train is much better than car – no parking, no highway stress
  • I would like to get a driver's licence / I have a driver's licence and find it very useful
⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • What are the advantages of public transport over cars? – Cheaper, eco-friendly, reduces congestion, you can read/relax, no parking stress.
  • What would you do to reduce traffic in cities? – Congestion charges, park & ride, better cycling infrastructure, subsidise public transport.
  • Is it fair to ban old diesel cars from city centres? – Yes: air quality improves. No: unfair to poorer people who can't afford new cars.
  • How do you usually travel and why? – Personal answer with specific reasons.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes Start with means of transport comparison. Focus on one or two in depth (car vs. train is a good contrast). Then talk about traffic problems in Czech Republic and your city. Mention environmental impact. End with your own transport habits and why you chose them.
