Culture and Mass Media

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • Types of media: TV, radio, newspapers, internet, social media, podcasts, streaming
  • Movies: genre vocab: thriller, comedy, documentary, sci-fi, animation
  • Music: genres, live concerts, festivals (Glastonbury, Colours of Ostrava)
  • Books: fiction vs non-fiction; e-books vs physical; reading habits
  • Social media pros/cons: connection vs addiction, fake news, filter bubble

Types of mass media

Print media
Newspapers, magazines. Declining but still trusted for in-depth journalism. MF Dnes, Hospodářské noviny, The Guardian.
Television
Still dominant for news and entertainment. ČT, Nova, Prima in CZ. BBC, ITV in UK.
Radio
Good for commuting. News (BBC Radio 4), music (Radio 1). Less visual distraction.
Internet/Social media
Fastest growing; personalised content. Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, X (Twitter). Issues: fake news, addiction.
Streaming
Netflix, HBO Max, Spotify – on-demand, subscription model. Replacing traditional TV.

Film

  • Genres: action, comedy, romance, thriller, horror, sci-fi, documentary, animation
  • Watching in Czech: dubbing vs subtitles (experts say subtitles are better for language learning)
  • My favourite film / director: describe plot briefly and why you like it
  • Famous Czech films: Kolja (Oscar 1997), Pelíšky, Samotáři; directors: Miloš Forman (One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest, Amadeus)

Music

  • Genres: pop, rock, jazz, classical, hip-hop, electronic, folk
  • Listening: Spotify, live concerts, YouTube
  • Major festivals: Glastonbury (UK, ~200,000 people), Colours of Ostrava (CZ), Rock for People (Hradec Králové)
  • Describe your musical taste and your last concert experience

Social media – pros and cons

AdvantagesDisadvantages
Stay connected with friends and familyAddiction; average user spends 2+ hours/day
Platform for creativity and self-expressionFake news and misinformation spread fast
Access to news and world events instantlyFilter bubble – you only see what algorithms show you
Career opportunities (LinkedIn, portfolio)Cyberbullying; mental health issues (teen girls)
⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • Do you think social media does more harm than good? – Present both sides. Conclude with nuanced view: depends on how you use it. Passive scrolling = harmful. Active creation/connection = beneficial.
  • Are newspapers dying? – Print circulation declining. But quality journalism still exists online (The Guardian, NYT). Issue: people don't pay for news online → quality suffers.
  • What is a filter bubble? – Social media algorithms show you content you already agree with. Result: you only see one point of view → political polarisation.
  • What is your favourite book and why? – Prepare a book description: genre, plot summary (without spoiling), what you liked, what it made you think about.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes Cover media types first. Then go deeper on one area you're comfortable with – film, music, or social media. For social media: show you can discuss it critically (not just list pros and cons, but argue a position). Always include personal examples – your favourite film, band, or how you use social media.
