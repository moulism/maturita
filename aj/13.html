Shopping and Money

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • Brick & mortar vs online shopping: pros and cons of each
  • Supermarkets, hypermarkets, shopping malls, market stalls, boutiques
  • Money: cash vs card vs contactless vs mobile pay; credit vs debit
  • Budget: income, expenses, savings, debt, pocket money, part-time job
  • Consumer culture: impulse buying, influencers, marketing tricks, overconsumption

Types of shops

supermarket
Large food store (Albert, Lidl, Tesco). One-stop shopping for groceries.
hypermarket
Very large store selling food + electronics + clothing (Kaufland, Globus).
shopping mall
Indoor complex with many stores, restaurants, cinema (OC Olympia in Plzeň).
market / market stall
Outdoor vendors – fresh produce, crafts, second-hand. Farmers' markets popular.
boutique
Small specialised shop, often with premium/unique products.

Online vs Brick-and-mortar shopping

Online (Amazon, Alza, Mall)Physical stores
PriceOften cheaper, easy to compareSometimes higher, but special offers
Convenience24/7, delivered homeCan see/touch product, immediate
ReturnsNeed to ship back (inconvenient)Take it back to store directly
Social aspectIsolatedSocial experience, browsing
EnvironmentPackaging waste, delivery trucksBetter if you walk/cycle there

Money – vocabulary

pocket money
Kapesné – regular small allowance from parents.
credit card
Borrow money from bank to buy now, pay later. Risk: debt accumulation.
debit card
Directly uses your own money from bank account.
budget
Rozpočet – planned income and expenses.
debt
Dluh – money owed. Consumer debt is a major problem in many countries.
impulse buying
Unplanned purchase driven by emotion or marketing. Marketers design stores to trigger this.

Marketing and consumer manipulation

  • Loss leaders: cheap products at entrance to draw you in (bread, milk)
  • Anchor pricing: showing original price crossed out to make sale price seem better
  • Influencers: paid social media promotion – often undisclosed. Young people most susceptible.
  • Artificial scarcity: "Only 2 left!" creates urgency
  • Free trials: Netflix, Spotify – subscriptions are hard to cancel
⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • What do you prefer: online or in-store shopping? – Personal preference with specific reasons. Avoid just 'it depends' – commit to a view.
  • Do you think young people spend too much money? – Yes: influenced by social media, peer pressure, FOMO. Financial literacy should be taught in schools.
  • What is the impact of fast fashion? – Environmental (textile waste, water use), social (low-wage workers in Bangladesh, India). Alternative: second-hand shops, capsule wardrobe.
  • Do you have a part-time job? Would you like one? – Describe experience if yes. Benefits: money, work experience, independence. Downside: less time for study.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes Start with types of shops and compare online vs physical. Then move to money vocabulary and your own habits. The interesting part is consumer psychology – how shops and marketers manipulate us. Examiners love when you're critical and aware. End with your personal shopping and money habits.
