Life Style

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • Healthy lifestyle: balanced diet, regular exercise, enough sleep, stress management
  • Unhealthy: fast food, sedentary life, alcohol, smoking, drugs, screen addiction
  • My routine: school, hobbies, sport, social life, sleep schedule
  • Mental health: stress, anxiety, depression; growing awareness among young people
  • Free time: sports, reading, gaming, music, social media, travel

Components of a healthy lifestyle

AreaHealthy choicesUnhealthy choices
DietBalanced: vegetables, fruit, protein, fibre; cooking at homeFast food, processed food, too much sugar and salt
Exercise150 min/week moderate activity recommended; sport, cycling, gymSedentary lifestyle – sitting 8+ hours a day
Sleep7–9 hours for teenagers/adults; regular bedtimeSleep deprivation → focus problems, mood swings, immune issues
StressMindfulness, hobbies, talking to friends, exerciseBottling up problems, overworking, social isolation
AddictionsAvoiding smoking, excess alcohol, drugsSmoking kills ~7M/year globally; alcohol – social norm but risky

Free time activities

  • Sport: football (global), ice hockey (CZ), swimming, cycling, gym – physical + mental health benefits
  • Hobbies: reading, gaming, music, cooking, photography – creativity and relaxation
  • Social life: spending time with friends, parties, dating – human connection is essential for wellbeing
  • Technology leisure: YouTube, gaming, social media – can be positive (learning, creativity) or negative (addiction)

My daily routine – what to say

  • I wake up at [time], have breakfast, travel to school by [transport]
  • After school I [sport / homework / hobby]
  • In the evenings I usually [relax, read, game, socialise]
  • My biggest challenge is [balancing school and free time / getting enough sleep]
  • I try to [exercise X times a week / eat healthily / reduce screen time]

Mental health – important to mention

Why mental health matters Awareness has grown significantly. 1 in 4 people will experience a mental health problem. Depression and anxiety are especially common among teenagers due to social media pressure, academic stress, and uncertainty about the future. Talking about it openly is important – reduces stigma.

Useful vocabulary

sedentary
Sedavý – involving a lot of sitting. A sedentary lifestyle increases the risk of heart disease.
mindfulness
Mindfulness – being present in the moment; meditation technique for stress reduction.
work-life balance
Rovnováha mezi prací a osobním životem. Increasingly important topic.
addiction
Závislost – compulsive need for a substance or behaviour.
⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • What do you do to stay healthy? – Specific personal answer: which sport, how often, what you eat. Avoid vague 'I try to eat healthy'.
  • Is social media bad for mental health? – Research shows Instagram use correlates with depression in teenage girls (internal Facebook research). But it can also provide community and support. Use critically.
  • What is your biggest vice / bad habit? – Be honest: too much screen time, not enough exercise, irregular sleep. Examiners respect self-awareness.
  • Do you think the work-life balance in the Czech Republic is good? – Compare to other countries. Czechs work long hours; Scandinavian countries have better balance. Discuss.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes Structure: what makes a healthy lifestyle (diet, exercise, sleep, mental health). Then describe YOUR lifestyle honestly – both good habits and areas to improve. Examiners are not judging you on whether you're healthy; they're assessing your ability to discuss the topic in English with genuine opinions.
