Information Technology

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • Devices: smartphone, laptop, tablet, smart TV, smartwatch, IoT devices
  • Functions: communication, work, entertainment, education, e-commerce, cloud storage
  • Social media: Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Facebook, LinkedIn, X (Twitter)
  • Negative impact: addiction, privacy, cyberbullying, fake news, health (posture, eyes)
  • Future: AI, automation, blockchain, metaverse, quantum computing

How technology has changed our lives

  • Communication: instant messaging (WhatsApp, Messenger), video calls (Zoom, Teams) – connected globally
  • Work: remote work possible, cloud storage (Google Drive, OneDrive), collaboration tools
  • Education: online courses (Coursera, YouTube tutorials), e-books, AI tutors
  • Shopping: e-commerce (Amazon, Alza), price comparison, user reviews
  • Healthcare: health apps, telemedicine, wearable health monitors

Negative impacts of technology

ProblemDetails
AddictionAverage person checks phone 150+ times/day. Dopamine-driven design in apps.
PrivacyBig data – companies collect massive personal data. GDPR (EU regulation) tries to protect users.
CyberbullyingOnline harassment – harder to escape than physical bullying (24/7). Causes depression, anxiety.
Fake newsSpreads faster than true news on social media. Hard to verify. Influences elections.
Digital divideGap between those with and without technology access – increases inequality.

Future technologies

Artificial Intelligence
AI can write, draw, code, diagnose diseases. ChatGPT, Midjourney. Will transform jobs.
Automation
Robots replacing repetitive jobs. ~30% of jobs at risk by 2030 (McKinsey). New jobs will emerge.
Cloud computing
Storing and processing data on remote servers (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud).
5G / 6G
Ultra-fast wireless networks. Enables autonomous vehicles, remote surgery, IoT at scale.
⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • Could you live without your smartphone for a week? – Honest answer. It reveals dependency. Most people find it very difficult – this itself illustrates the addiction point.
  • Is AI a threat or an opportunity? – Both. Opportunity: solves problems humans can't, improves medicine, education. Threat: job displacement, autonomous weapons, deepfakes, loss of privacy.
  • What is GDPR? – General Data Protection Regulation – EU law since 2018. Controls how companies collect and use personal data. You can request deletion of your data.
  • Is technology making people less intelligent? – Debate: outsourcing memory to Google, calculators for maths. Counter: frees up cognitive capacity for higher thinking; we use tools differently now.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes As an IT student, you have an advantage here – you can speak from personal experience and knowledge. Don't just list devices; discuss HOW technology changes society. Pick one area (AI, social media, privacy) and go deep. Show critical thinking: both benefits and risks.
