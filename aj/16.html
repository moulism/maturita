Teenagers' Problems

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • Family issues: communication with parents, different values, curfews, divorce
  • School: exam pressure, bullying, academic failure, choosing future path
  • Social: peer pressure, fitting in, social media comparison, FOMO
  • Mental health: anxiety, depression, eating disorders, self-harm
  • Addictions: alcohol, drugs, smoking, social media, gaming
  • Cyberbullying: 24/7 harassment, anonymity, screenshots last forever

Cyberbullying – why it's worse than traditional bullying

  • 24/7 – can't escape it at home, no safe space
  • Anonymity – bullies feel protected behind a screen – more cruel
  • Audience – hundreds of people can see and share the humiliation
  • Permanence – screenshots exist forever even if deleted
  • Solutions: report to platform, block, tell an adult, save evidence, involve police if serious

Peer pressure – how it works

  • Teenagers are biologically more susceptible to social approval and peer influence
  • Can be positive (friends encourage study, sport) or negative (drugs, skipping school)
  • Social media amplifies peer pressure: everyone looks perfect online (filters, edited photos)
  • FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out) drives anxiety about not being included

How to deal with problems

Talk about it
To a trusted adult, friend, school counsellor, or therapist. Silence makes it worse.
Exercise
Proven to reduce anxiety and depression. Even 30 minutes of walking helps.
Digital detox
Regular breaks from social media. Compare less, live more.
Seek help early
Mental health problems are easier to treat early. There is no shame in asking for help.
⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • What is the biggest challenge for teenagers today? – Mental health / social media pressure / academic stress. Explain specifically why (not just list).
  • Have you ever experienced peer pressure? – Be honest. Describe a situation and how you dealt with it. Shows maturity.
  • How has social media affected teenage mental health? – Correlation between Instagram use and depression in girls (leaked Facebook research). Constant comparison with unrealistic images. But also: online communities provide support.
  • Do you think the drinking age should be higher? – Czech Republic: 18 for all alcohol. USA: 21. Arguments for raising it: brain development, addiction. Arguments against: adults at 18 can vote/drive.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes This topic is personal – examiners want YOUR experience and opinions, not just facts. Talk about pressures you actually face or have faced (school stress, social media, peer pressure). Be thoughtful about mental health – show awareness and sensitivity. End with positive note: how young people can support each other and themselves.
