Travelling

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • Types: package holidays, backpacking, city break, ecotourism, adventure, staycation
  • Accommodation: hotel (1–5 stars), hostel, Airbnb, camping, B&B
  • Transport: plane, train (Interrail), ferry, car, bus
  • At the airport: check-in, boarding pass, gate, departure, arrival, customs, luggage
  • My experience: favourite destination, dream holiday
  • Overtourism: Prague, Barcelona, Venice – negative effects on locals

Types of holidays

Package holiday
Organised all-inclusive trip (flights + hotel + transfers). Easy but less flexible.
Backpacking
Independent travel with a backpack, budget accommodation (hostels). Freedom, adventure.
City break
Short trip (2–4 days) to a city. Prague, Vienna, Barcelona popular destinations.
Ecotourism
Travel focused on nature and sustainability. Minimises environmental impact.
Adventure tourism
Hiking, bungee jumping, rafting, skiing. Active holidays.
Staycation
Holiday at home or locally. Became popular during COVID-19.

Airport vocabulary

check-in
Registrace na let – confirm your seat, drop off luggage.
boarding pass
Palubní lístek – ticket to board the plane.
departure/arrival
Odlet/přilet – boards show both.
customs
Celnice – border control where goods are checked.
hand luggage
Příruční zavazadlo – bag you take on the plane.

Overtourism

  • Prague: 8 million tourists/year (population 1.3M). Locals priced out of centre. Tourist traps. Noise pollution in residential areas.
  • Venice, Barcelona: similar problems. Barcelona banned new tourist apartments. Venice charging entry fee from 2024.
  • Solutions: spread tourism to less known destinations, seasonal pricing, limit tourist accommodation in city centres.
⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • What is overtourism and how can it be solved? – Too many tourists damaging a destination (Prague, Venice). Solutions: spread visitors to less known places, price controls, limit tourist accommodation.
  • Is budget travel (Ryanair, hostels) good or bad? – Good: democratises travel, people experience other cultures. Bad: increases flight emissions, contributes to overtourism.
  • Describe your best/most memorable holiday. – Specific details – where, when, who with, what you did, what surprised you, what you learned.
  • What is your dream holiday destination and why? – Be specific – not just 'I want to go to Japan' but WHY – what specifically interests you.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes Open with types of holidays and ask yourself which you prefer. Then describe the practicalities: booking, airport experience, accommodation. Include your own experiences – best holiday, places you've been. Talk about overtourism – it shows critical thinking. End with your dream destination – examiners enjoy this discussion.
