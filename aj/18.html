Food and Drinks

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • My diet: what I eat daily, healthy vs unhealthy habits
  • Czech cuisine: svíčková, knedlíky, bramboráky, trdelník (tourist trap!), Pilsner
  • British cuisine: fish and chips, full English breakfast, Sunday roast, tea culture
  • Eating out: restaurant, fast food, takeaway, café; tipping culture
  • Food trends: veganism, vegetarianism, organic, local, gluten-free

Czech traditional cuisine

  • Svíčková – beef sirloin in creamy vegetable sauce with bread dumplings (knedlíky), cranberry sauce, and whipped cream
  • Vepřo-knedlo-zelo – roast pork + dumplings + sauerkraut; typical Sunday lunch
  • Guláš – beef stew with paprika; influenced by Hungarian cuisine
  • Bramboráky – potato pancakes; popular street food at markets
  • Trdelník – actually NOT traditional Czech! It's a Hungarian/Slovak dish commercialised for tourists in Prague.
  • Pilsner beer – invented in Plzeň 1842; Czech Republic has highest beer consumption per capita worldwide

British cuisine

  • Fish and chips – battered fried fish + thick chips; national dish; served with mushy peas and vinegar
  • Full English breakfast – eggs (fried/scrambled), bacon, sausages, baked beans, toast, mushrooms, tomatoes
  • Sunday roast – roast beef/chicken/lamb + roast potatoes + Yorkshire pudding + vegetables + gravy
  • Tea culture – ~165 million cups per day in UK. Builder's tea = strong black tea with milk and sugar.
  • Scones – with clotted cream and jam (cream tea); part of British afternoon tea tradition

My diet and eating habits

  • I usually have [breakfast: cereal/toast/yoghurt / I skip breakfast]
  • For lunch I [eat at school canteen / bring packed lunch / go to a restaurant]
  • Dinner is [family meal / quick something]
  • I like [favourite foods] and I dislike [foods you don't like]
  • I try to [eat healthier / reduce sugar / eat more vegetables]

Food trends and vocabulary

vegan
Eats no animal products at all (no meat, dairy, eggs, honey).
vegetarian
No meat but may eat dairy and eggs.
organic food
Grown without synthetic pesticides or fertilisers. More expensive but fewer chemicals.
food waste
~1/3 of all food produced globally is wasted. Environmental and ethical issue.
takeaway
Food ordered to take home or eat elsewhere. Fast food, pizza delivery.
⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • Is Czech cuisine healthy? – Traditional Czech food is heavy – lots of pork, dumplings, cream sauces. High in carbs and fat. But also vegetables, soups. Modern Czech cuisine is lighter.
  • What do you think about veganism? – Environmental benefits (meat = high emissions). Health: can be healthy with proper planning. Cultural: food is tied to identity and tradition. Respect both sides.
  • Is fast food a problem? – Cheap and convenient. But high in sugar, salt, saturated fat → obesity, diabetes. Disproportionately affects lower-income communities.
  • What is your favourite Czech dish and could you make it? – Personal answer. Describe how it's made briefly. Shows cultural pride.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes Start with Czech cuisine – show you know your own culture (mention Pilsner was invented in Plzeň – examiner will be impressed). Compare briefly with British food. Then discuss eating habits personally – honest about fast food and healthy eating. Mention one food trend (veganism, organic) with your opinion on it.
