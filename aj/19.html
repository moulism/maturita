Personality and Family Relationships

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • Personality traits: extrovert/introvert, ambitious, reliable, creative, empathetic, stubborn
  • Family structure: nuclear family, extended family, single-parent, blended family
  • Relationships: parents, siblings, grandparents, friends; generation gap
  • Describing people: appearance + character; physical vs personality description
  • Conflict resolution: communication, compromise, listening, empathy

Personality traits – vocabulary

extrovert
Energised by being around others. Outgoing, talkative. Vs introvert: prefers alone time to recharge.
ambitious
Ambiciózní – has strong desire to achieve goals.
reliable
Spolehlivý – can be counted on to do what they say.
empathetic
Empatický – able to understand and share feelings of others.
stubborn
Tvrdohlavý – refuses to change opinion even when wrong.
impulsive
Impulzivní – acts quickly without thinking about consequences.
optimist/pessimist
Optimista/pesimista – sees the glass half full / half empty.

Describing yourself – what to say

  • I would describe myself as [trait] because [specific example].
  • My biggest strength is [analytical thinking / creativity / reliability].
  • My weakness is [impatience / disorganisation / overthinking] but I try to [improve].
  • My friends would describe me as [honest / funny / loyal / helpful].

Family structure

TypeDescriptionTrend
Nuclear familyTwo parents + children (traditional model)Still common but decreasing
Single-parent familyOne parent raising children (often after divorce)Increasing – ~35% in CZ
Extended familyMultiple generations living togetherMore common in Asia, less in Europe
Blended familyPartners with children from previous relationshipsIncreasing due to higher divorce rates
Same-sex familyTwo parents of same genderLegal in many countries; CZ: registered partnership but not marriage yet

My family – what to talk about

  • Describe family members briefly: relationship + one personality trait each
  • Family traditions: what do you do together? (Christmas dinner, summer holidays)
  • Generation gap: what do you disagree about with your parents?
  • Important family value: respect, honesty, support, education
⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • Are you more like your mother or father? – Pick one and give specific personality examples, not just 'we look similar'.
  • What qualities do you look for in a friend? – Loyalty, honesty, sense of humour, reliability. Explain which is most important to you and why.
  • Has the role of women in Czech families changed? – Yes: more women work, more equal division of household duties. But Czech Republic still below EU average for gender equality at work.
  • Do you think the generation gap is bigger today than 50 years ago? – Technology creates huge difference – digital natives vs digital immigrants. But some values (honesty, hard work) are timeless.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes Personality is the most personal topic – use lots of first person ('I am', 'I think', 'I believe'). Describe yourself with specific examples, not just adjectives. For family: describe your own family briefly, then discuss broader trends (divorce rates, changing family structures). Have an opinion on family values you consider important.
