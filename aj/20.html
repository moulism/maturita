Living – Home and Housing

⏱ ~15 minutes
📝 Key points – write on paper
  • Types: house (detached, semi-detached, terraced), flat/apartment, studio, cottage
  • City vs countryside: pros and cons; trends (urbanisation vs urban flight)
  • My home: describe room by room; what you would change
  • Housing problems: high prices, renting vs buying, homelessness
  • Household chores: who does what, fairness between family members

Types of housing

detached house
Rodinný dům (standalone). Most space, garden, private, expensive.
semi-detached
Dvojdomek – house sharing one wall with a neighbour. Common in UK.
terraced house
Řadový dům – row of houses sharing walls. Typical in British cities.
flat / apartment
Byt – unit in a larger building. Common in cities. Cheaper to buy/rent.
studio flat
Garsoniéra – single room (kitchen + living + bedroom combined). For singles.
cottage
Chalupa – rural holiday home. Very popular in Czech Republic (chatky, chaty).

City vs Countryside

CityCountryside
AdvantagesJobs, public transport, culture, shops, universitiesFresh air, nature, quiet, cheaper housing, community
DisadvantagesExpensive, noisy, polluted, crowded, stressfulLimited jobs, poor transport, far from services
TrendUrbanisation: 56% of world now lives in citiesPost-COVID: "urban flight" – remote workers move out

Housing problems in Czech Republic

  • High prices: Prague among most expensive cities relative to local salaries in EU. Average flat costs ~15 years of average salary.
  • Renting vs buying: Czech tradition is to own; but young people increasingly can't afford to buy → forced to rent
  • Homelessness: ~30,000 homeless in CZ; often caused by job loss, addiction, mental illness, domestic violence
  • Housing for elderly: insufficient places in care homes; many families care for elderly relatives at home

My home – how to describe it

  • I live in a [flat / house / apartment block] in [area of Plzeň/your town]
  • My home has [number] rooms: [living room, kitchen, bedrooms, bathroom]
  • My favourite place is my bedroom because [reason]
  • I would change [something] – e.g. more space, better view, garden
  • The most important thing in a home to me is [comfort / location / space for hobbies]

⚠ Typical examiner questions
  • Would you rather live in a city or the countryside? – Commit to one with 3 specific reasons. Mention the other side briefly to show balance.
  • Can young people afford to buy a home in Czech Republic today? – Generally no – prices have tripled in 10 years. Mortgage interest rates rose sharply in 2022–2024. Generation renting is becoming the norm.
  • Who does the household chores in your family? Is it fair? – Honest answer. Discuss whether the division is fair and along gender lines.
  • What would your dream home look like? – Describe specifically – location, type of building, garden, interior. This is a fun question; be imaginative.
💡 How to speak for 15 minutes Describe types of housing first with Czech context (pannelák = prefab block from communist era, very common). Compare city vs countryside with your own preference clearly stated. Talk about housing problems – young people can't afford to buy is a real issue students relate to. End with your own home and dream home.
